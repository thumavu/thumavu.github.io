The problem

South Africa is among the leading countries with the worst alcohol-related fatalities in the world, according to the 2015 Global Status Report on Road Safety

Research done by Arrive Alive between March 2015 and March 2016 indicates that 50% of the people who lose their lives on South African roads contain an alcohol concentration of about 0.5g per 100ml of blood.

Solution

The idea came when we were attending the hacktheworld hackathon which was held in Johannesburg July 29, 2017. We were part of the top ten for our idea and we decided to move forward and implement the product.

The approach we took was prevention before cure. By this we decided that the application should be educational and informative thus giving power to the user, informing him/her about his/her drinking habits.

What happens is, the user breaths in the breathalyser, the breathalyser then process the information it is fed, then sends the information wirelessly to the mobile application of the user. The app then does more calculation of the BAC(blood alcohol concentration) of the user, then informs the user of his/his alcohol level, according to this information. The application suggests uber and/or taxify for the user. It also sends the information along with the location to the user's next of keen should it be necessary. Over time the user can access the data about his/her drinking pattens to be able to do informed decisions and take measures.
